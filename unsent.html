drafts that never left

Seven messages. None of them delivered. Some of them still true.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: the version of myself from seven years ago

You're going to be okay. Not in the way you're hoping, but in a way that turns out to be better.

You will get used to not knowing. The uncertainty doesn't end — you just stop needing it to.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: the building on the old street

I still think about the way the light came through the kitchen window at 4pm in October. A particular gold.

I don't think anyone who lives there now notices it. It comes and goes without comment.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: the idea I had at 2am on a Tuesday

I wrote "remember this" in my phone, but not what to remember. I'm sorry.

You may have been important. The window was probably brief.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: the last ordinary day

I didn't know you were the last one.

I spent most of you on small things — a long lunch, a walk that went nowhere, half a film I didn't finish. I think that was right.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: everyone I didn't reply to in time

I was going to. I had the words. I was saving them for the right moment, which turned out to be the moment after the window closed.

I still owe you something. I'm not sure how to pay it back.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: the feeling I had walking home that night

I was trying to name you the whole way back. You weren't sadness, not exactly. Not longing either.

I gave up and went to bed. You were gone by morning. I've been looking for you since.

DRAFT — UNSENT TO: the earlier draft of this

You were more honest. You said too much in the wrong order and couldn't be sent.

This version is more careful. It is less true. The usual tradeoff.
