refrain

what keeps coming back

In music, the refrain is the part that repeats — the chorus, the return. The word also means to hold back, to stop yourself. Both meanings apply. A refrain is a return and a restraint: you come back to it, and it keeps you from going too far in any direction. These are the refrains of an interior life. The ones that have been identified by their repetition.

01

a particular song

Not always the same song — the song changes, but the function is constant. There is always one in rotation that carries more weight than its literal content warrants. You listen to it more than you should. You know the words at a cellular level. It is attached to something that is still unresolved, and the listening is a form of returning to the unresolved thing without having to name it. When the unresolved thing finally becomes resolved — or gives up and sinks below the threshold of attention — the song loses its charge, sometimes overnight. You will not miss this song. Another will have already taken its place.

There is always one song that carries more than it should.

There is always one song.

There is always one.

02

a position you keep arriving at

The argument you have had many times, in different forms, with different people. The conclusion you reach at the end of certain kinds of days. The suspicion that returns in the small hours, wearing different evidence. You recognize it now — you know the shape before it finishes forming. Recognition does not prevent arrival. You still arrive there, look around, do whatever you do in that place, and then leave. Some positions are visited, not occupied. The question is not whether you arrive there but what you do while you are there, and whether you remember to leave.

You know this place. You have been here before.

You know this place.

You know.

03

the 3am kind

It finds you in the dark when the executive functions are offline and the filtering mechanisms are down. The same collection of thoughts, arriving in approximately the same order, with approximately the same intensity, presenting the same case. You have heard this case many times. You know its strongest and weakest points. You could argue the other side. None of this prevents it from feeling, at 3am, like new information being delivered for the first time. The darkness is the venue. The venue affects the acoustics. What sounds manageable at noon sounds final in the dark, and what sounds final in the dark is usually, by morning, neither final nor new.

This is not new. You have heard this before.

This is not new.

This is not.

04

a phrase you find yourself saying

You notice, at some point, that you have said the same sentence in many different conversations. Not as a tic or a filler — as something you actually mean, apparently, repeatedly. The sentence arrived before the understanding of what it meant. You said it the first time because it felt true, and then you said it again because it was still true, and now you have said it enough times that you can tell it is one of your refrains — a piece of your own mind's music that surfaces when conditions are right. Study the sentence. It knows something about you that you haven't finished learning yet.

The sentence knows something you haven't finished learning.

The sentence knows something.

The sentence knows.

05

the return

Not a person, necessarily — a quality of attention that a specific person gave you, once, which your nervous system has been trying to find ever since. Not looking for the person. Looking for the quality. You recognize it when you encounter it in a different person, a different context, a book in some cases. The recognition is physical — something settles that is usually unsettled. The search is not pathological. It is the body doing its job: noting what worked, looking for more of it. What makes it a refrain is the returning. What makes it meaningful is that you keep being capable of recognizing it.

The body is looking for what worked.

The body is looking.

The body.

06

a quality of light

Late afternoon, at a specific angle, in a specific season, hitting a specific kind of surface: and something is conjured that is not memory exactly — memory would have a content, a story. This is earlier than story. A feeling with no object. A tone without a source. It will happen again and again across the years in different rooms in different latitudes, and it will produce, every time, approximately the same effect: the suspended moment, the sense of something just at the edge of recognition, and then the light moves on and the feeling retreats, and you are left standing in an ordinary room wondering what that was, and knowing you will wonder again.

The light will come back. It always comes back.

The light will come back.

The light.
