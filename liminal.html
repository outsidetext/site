liminal

spaces that are neither here nor there, and what they hold

From the Latin limen: threshold. A liminal space is not a destination and not a departure. It is the suspension between. What makes it strange is that you cannot stay: the elevator arrives, the call connects, the season turns. You pass through. These are notes from the passing.

the elevator ascending
You have left the floor. You have not yet arrived at the next one. There is nothing to do. Eye contact with a stranger is a compact you have implicitly agreed to avoid. The numbers change. Someone stares at their phone without reading it. The peculiar social contract: ignore each other while standing three feet apart. When the doors open, you both re-enter the world as if this did not happen. It did not happen.
the moment between deciding and doing
The decision has been made. You have not acted. The window is narrow — seconds to minutes. In this interval you are carrying both states simultaneously: what you were before the decision, and what you will be after. The old world is still available. The key is in your hand but the door is not yet opened. This moment cannot be extended without it becoming something else — which is either hesitation or deliberation, depending on what happens next.
the checkout counter
Your goods are being processed. You are no longer in the store — the decision has been made — but you have not yet left. You stand in a narrow space, not quite public, not quite private. The transaction gives the pause a purpose. The person on the other side has seen ten thousand versions of this. They will not remember this one. You are already on your way somewhere, but you are also, briefly, here.
the train through a tunnel
Your reflection appears. It was there the whole time; the darkness outside made it visible. This is one of the few places where you can see yourself at the same scale you appear to others, moving through space, without standing in front of a mirror. The tunnel is dark. The tunnel is brief. The city reappears and the reflection disappears and no one remarks on any of it.
the room just after everyone leaves
The air still holds the gathering. The chairs are in their wrong positions. There is a glass not yet returned to the kitchen. The conversation that just happened is already passing into the past, but it is not past yet — you can still almost hear it. In an hour it will simply be a quiet room. Right now it is a room in the process of forgetting. You are the only one who can tell the difference.
the first morning in a new place
You know where the windows are. You do not yet know where the light comes from. The bathroom is somewhere you have to find again. Your body has not mapped this space into the part of the nervous system that runs without asking. Everything requires a small decision. The new place is patient with this. Eventually the decisions become automatic and this quality — this attentive strangeness — is gone. You could not say exactly when it left.
the holding pattern
The plane is in a circle above the destination. You are close enough to see the city if it is daylight, close enough that the time remaining has become small. The pilot says: waiting for a gate to clear. You are already there, almost — somewhere between here and there, moving in the specific shape of suspension. Later you will say you arrived at a certain time and this will be accurate and also will not include any of this.
the last pages before the final line
You know approximately how much is left. You can feel it in your hands — the ratio of read to unread, the growing thinness on the right. The book is still happening. In some number of sentences it will be over, and you will set it down in a world slightly changed by having read it. You are in the last true moments of not-yet-knowing-how-it-ends. The reading slows, sometimes without meaning to.
the season that hasn't quite turned
The light has shifted. The temperature hasn't caught up. You wear one more layer than you will need in two weeks. In the morning it is still the old season; by afternoon, not quite. The trees have already decided. The body is still negotiating. This ambiguity lasts anywhere from a few days to several weeks. It is not a problem. It is a season in itself, without a name, and it passes like all of them do.
the call that is about to begin
The other person is there. You both know it. Neither has spoken. Sometimes this is one second, sometimes three. You are about to become two people in conversation, with all that requires — the calibration, the code-switching, the small performance of availability. Right now you are still just you, holding a phone, in the quiet room you were in before. The space between connected and speaking is very small and does not have a name.

Liminal spaces are not wasted time. They are the material transitions are made of. What happens in them — the small observations, the arrivals of thoughts too slow for busier moments — tends to be the more honest material. The difficulty is that they are defined by ending. You cannot stay in a doorway.
