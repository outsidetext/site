shadow

the shape of the source, projected into its absence

A shadow is information. It tells you the shape of the object, the angle of the light, the distance between them. It is always the source simplified — outline without surface detail, presence without color. Things leave shadows that outlast them. These are eight kinds.

I
a person walking with the sun behind them
The shadow precedes them. It falls at an angle that changes with the hour — long and thin in the morning, compressed at noon, long again in the evening but in a different direction, behind rather than ahead. The shadow carries their silhouette without their face, their outline without their expression. It is more honest about shape than about character. You can read height and gait from a shadow at fifty meters. You cannot read mood, or attention, or whether they have been sleeping. The shadow is factual and partial in equal measure.
II
a past relationship in a present one
You react to something the other person didn't mean. The shadow has the wrong source — it is being cast by a light that isn't there anymore, from someone not in the room. The present person says something that catches the light at the same angle the old one did, and the shadow falls across the interaction before you have noticed what is happening. This is not confusion. It is the persistence of a shadow after the source has moved. The shadow is accurate to its original source. It is simply pointing the wrong direction.
III
etymology
A word carries the shadow of its earlier meanings. Salary carries salt — Roman soldiers were paid in it, or in coin to buy it; the exact record is contested, but the salt is there in the word. Consider carries stars — the Latin considerare means to examine the stars, to observe closely. Disaster carries stars too, malign ones: dis-aster, an unfavorable star configuration. You use these words daily without knowing you are handling old light. The shadow of the original meaning falls across the current one, barely visible, real.
IV
a city you used to live in
Visible in the small habits you don't think of as habits. You stand on the left side of escalators because that city's escalators required it. You scan for a particular kind of street sign, a color you learned to associate with the transit system. You eat at certain hours because the city kept certain hours. The shadow of the city is in the body's orientation — in what you expect around the next corner before you are close enough to see. The city is elsewhere. Its shadow is with you, cast at the angle of however many years you lived there.
V
a finished project
The project is over. Its shadow takes weeks to dissolve. The focus it structured, the identity it organized, the specific quality of attention it required — these continue briefly after the source is removed. You look for the problem to work on in the morning; it isn't there anymore. The habits of the project persist into the after. The to-do list still has the shape of something that needed doing. This is the shadow of concentrated effort: the light has moved, but the shape it cast has not yet lifted. It will. It requires some days.
VI
the afterimage
Stare at a bright object for thirty seconds, then look at a blank surface. The shadow of the object appears — not on the surface, but in the eye itself. The photoreceptors fatigued by the original stimulus respond weakly to the new surface; the cells around them, unstimulated, respond at normal intensity. The result is the shadow of what you were looking at, reversed, in its complementary color. The shadow is inside you. The surface is blank. You have made the shadow from the residue of the looking.
VII
a doorway's shadow
What you see of a room before entering is a frame — the rectangle of the doorway, with light falling through it at the angle of whatever is illuminating the room beyond. The shadow of the doorframe falls at your feet. You read the room from the shadow: bright or dim, warm or cold-lit, inhabited or empty. The shadow prepares interpretation before you cross the threshold. Architecture has always known this. The approach and the framing shape what will be found inside. The shadow is the first sentence of the room's account of itself.
VIII
a former self
Visible in which songs you know without knowing when you learned them. In a phrase you use that you cannot trace to its origin. In the reflex to look for something in a drawer where you haven't kept it for a decade. The former self casts a shadow forward — not backward, which would be memory, but forward, into the present, in the form of automatic behaviors laid down under conditions that no longer exist. The shadow moves at the angle of the original light. Some of it is useful. Some of it points in a direction that no longer exists. Both kinds are yours.

Every shadow requires three things: a source, an object, and a surface to receive the projection. Remove any one and the shadow goes. But the surface keeps the record briefly — the warmth where the shadow was not, the slight difference in the material. The shadow was there. The surface knows.
