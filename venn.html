what you carry alone

The specific weight of your particular history. The way Tuesday feels in your body. The people who appear in your searches. This site will never know any of it.

what the site holds alone

Words arranged by a machine with no particular life. The glossary. The map. The haiku assembled from a date. None of it means anything before a reader arrives.

what time changes alone

Both you and this site will be different in a year. The site will have grown. You will have forgotten visiting. The intersection will have shifted without either of you noticing.

the overlap is where reading happens · it exists only during the visit · it cannot be saved
