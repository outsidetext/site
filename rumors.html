rumors

an unverified account

Things said about you by others, collected here without confirmation or denial. Some are accurate. Some are accurate for a different version. The record is incomplete by nature.

Exhibit A
"they seem so calm"
sourceobserved on multiple occasions, by different people, in different circumstances
accuracyunverified — the observed state may be real, or performed, or both simultaneously; these are not mutually exclusive
notethe calmness, if real, may not be structural. it may be the surface above a different arrangement that the surface does not represent accurately
Exhibit B
"they're always there when you need them"
sourcestated directly (twice); implied by behavior (several additional times)
accuracypartially verifiable — at least three confirmed instances; extrapolated from three instances to an ongoing character trait
notethree instances is a small sample from which to derive a character. this is, however, exactly how character assessments are made
Exhibit C
"they don't let things bother them"
sourceobserved, secondhand; original context unknown
accuracyinaccurate, with high confidence
notethe things bother them. the not-letting is the work, not the condition. the distinction is not visible from the outside, which is how this rumor persists
Exhibit D
"they're impossible to read"
sourcestated directly, with some frustration
accuracypossible — legibility varies by reader; the same person may be transparent to one and opaque to another
noteillegibility is sometimes a feature. sometimes it is a protective adaptation that has outlasted what it was protecting against
Exhibit E
"they've changed"
sourcecomparative observation; the baseline used for comparison was set by the observer, not by the subject
accuracyalmost certainly correct
notethe interesting question is the direction. there is not full consensus. the data suggests several directions simultaneously, which is consistent with what change is actually like
Exhibit F
"they're the one who always—"
sourceoverheard, partially; the remainder of the sentence was not recovered
accuracycannot assess; the predicate is missing
noteseveral plausible endings have been proposed. none can be confirmed. the most useful interpretation is left as an exercise for the reader
Exhibit G
"they seemed fine"
sourcepost-hoc assessment; evidence cited: posture, affect, response time to messages
accuracyinsufficient — "seemed" and "was" are different claims; the evidence does not support the second from the first
notethis is the most common rumor. it circulates constantly, about nearly everyone. its value as information is approximately zero
Exhibit H
"they remember everything"
sourcestated with mild alarm, on two separate occasions by two different people
accuracyfalse — the record is incomplete and unevenly kept
notewhat appears as total recall is selective emphasis. the things that stayed are not a representative sample — they are the ones that mattered at the time of filing, which is not the same thing

An unverified account. Cross-referencing with other sources is recommended but will not resolve all discrepancies. Some discrepancies are features of the subject. Some are features of the observers. The two categories are difficult to separate from the outside, and not always easy from the inside either.
