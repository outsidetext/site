migration

seven things that move between people without being carried

a phrase

Somewhere, someone says something a particular way — a construction, a rhythm, a word used slightly wrong and therefore more precisely right. It passes to the next person through exposure. No lesson was given. No decision was made to adopt it. Over time it is in more mouths, attributed to no one, owned by everyone who uses it and no one who first said it. The phrase has migrated. The person who first said it may not even recognize it now, in its distributed form, as the thing they said.

a gesture

Your mother had a way of tilting her head to the left when she was uncertain — not quite a question, not quite a no, something in the narrow space between them. You didn't decide to learn it. You notice your hands are doing something similar now, in a conversation where you are also uncertain. Not the head tilt: your version. But the same family. Something migrated across the years between you and arrived, unannounced, in your body. It doesn't require her presence. It has fully colonized you.

a mood

One person enters a room with a particular internal state, and the room eventually shares it. This is not metaphor and not infection; it is something more structural — how bodies read bodies, how the nervous system responds to the nervous system of others, below the threshold of language or intention. The mood doesn't announce itself. It is not transmitted deliberately. The person carrying it may not know they have it. An hour later everyone in the room has been somewhere they didn't choose to go.

a musical preference

A song was not yours. You heard it in someone else's apartment, in the context of their life, their morning, their specific relationship to that particular sound. You heard it enough times that the association loosened from them and reattached to you — to your mornings, your context, your use. The song didn't change. The carrier changed. It now lives in you as freely as it ever lived in them, and you have no strong memory of the handoff. The preference migrated at some point during proximity and you only noticed it after the proximity ended.

a fear

Fear propagates through populations because populations that shared fear survived. A single animal detects a threat and the surrounding animals respond before they have seen what the first one saw — responding to the response, which is faster than responding to the thing. The fear migrates via behavior before it migrates via information. In human beings this works across larger distances and longer time scales: fears transmitted between generations without ever being named as such, arriving as dispositions, as aversions, as the feeling that certain things should not be touched or said or wanted. The fear migrates. The reason it migrated gets lost.

a pace

You begin walking slower after extended time with someone who walks slowly. You didn't decide to match them. Something adjusted, and the adjustment persisted after the walks ended. The same thing happens with speech — you find yourself using a particular cadence that was not yours, speaking at an interval that was someone else's rhythm. You carry the pace of people you have been close to. The body takes notes on other bodies without asking permission, and the notes outlast the relationship.

a kind of attention

Some people teach you, by example, that certain things are worth looking at longer. They pause in front of something — a window, a sentence, a sound — and the pause says: this is worth your time. You are changed by this. You begin pausing in similar places, but for your own reasons, which have now grown distinct from theirs. The attention itself migrated: the capacity to find something in that category of thing. Later you give this to others without knowing it. The thing someone paused in front of decades ago has become part of how you move through the world, distributed onward, origin untraceable.

Note: migration of this kind requires no permission and leaves no record. You cannot inventory what you have received or given. You are already carrying more than you know.
