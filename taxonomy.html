taxonomy

a scientific classification of silences

all silences are not the same silence. what follows is an attempt at a formal system of classification. the taxonomy is incomplete. the subject resists comprehensive treatment.

KINGDOM: Experiential  ·  PHYLUM: Acoustic  ·  CLASS: varies by specimen

Class I — Silences That Follow Something
Silentium posterga
the silence after someone has left a room

occupies the space recently vacated by sound. characterized by a precise negative shape corresponding to the person who departed. duration: 15–90 seconds in mild cases; indefinite if the departure is final. the silence is not quiet; it has the texture of what was just said.

range: doorways, hallways, the end of phone calls
Silentium argumenti
the silence after an argument

denser than ordinary silence. contains unspoken remainders from both sides of the exchange. objects in the room do not participate but are complicit. duration: variable; measured not in minutes but in the number of times one person considers speaking and doesn't.

range: kitchens, cars, rooms where important conversations happen
Silentium musici
the silence before the applause

a rare and collectively held specimen. lasts between 0.5 and 3 seconds. contains the entire performance just ended. the audience, without coordination, agrees to remain inside it for one shared moment before returning to individual experience. among the most fragile silences known; ruptures on contact.

range: concert halls, theaters, the last note of any song played well
Class II — Silences That Wait
Silentium expectationis
the silence while waiting for someone to speak

has direction. oriented toward the person expected to break it. distinct from ordinary quiet in that it has a subject. the silence presses gently on the one who is expected to fill it. can be weaponized. more often simply occurs, without intent.

range: first dates, difficult conversations, waiting rooms, any pause longer than three seconds
Silentium responsi
the silence after sending a message

exists in the interval between transmission and receipt. closely related to latency (q.v.) but distinguished by its emotional charge. the silence is passive on the part of the environment and active on the part of the sender. duration: variable, range: seconds to never. both ends of the range have been documented.

range: phones, inboxes, the space between send and reply
Class III — Silences That Arrive Without Cause
Silentium diei
the silence of three in the afternoon

not absolute quiet — the world continues. but a particular quality of attention becomes available at this hour that is not available at others. the silence is not in the room but in the noticing. arrives unsought. departs before anything can be done with it. only documented retrospectively.

range: mid-afternoon, particularly in rooms with west-facing windows
Silentium solitudinis
the silence of being alone in a place that is usually full

compound specimen: contains both the current absence of others and the memory of their presence. the room retains its social architecture — chairs arranged for conversation, cups that imply former company — while being empty. most intense when encountered unexpectedly.

range: family homes between visits, offices on weekends, any shared space encountered alone
Class IV — Silences That Mean Something
Silentium verbi
the silence instead of the word

a deliberate specimen. the word was available. the silence was chosen instead. carries the full weight of the word it replaced, with additional meaning from the choosing. can indicate: love, grief, the exact right word arriving too late, agreement too complicated to be spoken, the thing that does not survive being said.

range: all contexts; particularly dense at important moments
Silentium ultimum
the last ordinary silence

indistinguishable from other silences at the time of occurrence. only reclassified in retrospect, when it becomes clear that what followed changed the context of everything before it. the silence itself is unchanged by the reclassification. this is characteristic of the type.

range: everywhere; recognized only after the fact

note: this taxonomy is a working document. several classes remain uncharted. the silence inside a sealed letter. the silence of a name no longer used. the silence that is actually a held breath. classification continues.
