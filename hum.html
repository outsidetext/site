A room can hum. Buildings hum at their resonant frequencies — the particular pitch at which their structure vibrates most naturally. Power lines hum at 60hz in North America, 50hz in Europe. The human body has its own resonant frequencies: the thorax at approximately 5hz, the abdomen at 4–8hz, the eyeballs at around 19hz.

Below 20hz, sound becomes vibration rather than hearing. The border between the two senses dissolves. You stop hearing it and start being it.

The note A4 is tuned to 440hz by international agreement since 1939. Before that: 432hz, 435hz, 423hz, sometimes higher, sometimes lower, depending on where you were and when. The standard is arbitrary. So is every other standard.

When two people hum together without planning to, they often find the same note within a few seconds. The body knows how to harmonize before the mind catches up.
