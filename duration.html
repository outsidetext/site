duration

how long things last — approximate measurements

Some things are measured easily. Others require different instruments: the body's own clock, or the recurrence of a thought, or the number of times a thing has to happen before it stops being surprising.

of the ordinary

things that pass through regularly and leave

coffee at the exact right temperature 4–6 minutes window between scalding and merely warm; narrower than memory suggests
a song stuck in your head before it finally releases minutes to 4 days highly variable; the more you try to dislodge it, the longer it holds
the mood left by a dream after waking 20–90 minutes lingers in the body after the content has dissolved; the feeling stays after the story goes
good news before it becomes ordinary 30 min – 3 days the gap between receiving and integrating; shorter for smaller goods
the feeling after sending an important message 10 min – several hours depends on whether a reply is expected; the waiting extends the feeling
the impression a room makes when you first walk in under 3 seconds registered before any conscious analysis; often accurate; difficult to override once set

of the heavier kind

things that do not leave on a predictable schedule

grief after a minor daily loss 3–8 hours, recurring small griefs do not accumulate proportionally; they recur at their original size
a moment of genuine happiness, noticed while still happening 40 sec – 4 minutes after which the noticing itself begins to change it; observation shortens the observation
the body's memory of a house you no longer live in years; partial; indefinite hands remember the light switches; feet remember the step down into the kitchen; the specific sounds last longest
the reflex to share something with a person you're no longer in contact with months to years diminishing but recurring; the impulse arrives before the knowledge that there is nowhere to send it
embarrassment, acute 1–20 minutes, then periodic the acute phase passes; the memory visits without warning for years afterward, often at night
missing someone acute: variable · chronic: indefinite acute phase resolves with proximity or acceptance; chronic phase runs at lower intensity, dormant between activations

of the instant and the permanent

things measured in a different unit entirely

the exact right word, arriving too late instantaneous; permanent the arrival is instant; the awareness that it arrived late is a condition, not an event
the precise moment a relationship changes a second; recognized later the duration of the moment is brief; the duration of understanding it is years
the knowledge that something is over arrives once; does not repeat some knowledge arrives fully formed and cannot be unknown; duration not applicable — it is simply now part of the permanent record

The interesting thing about duration is that it is not felt as time while it is happening. You do not feel the 4 minutes of the coffee cooling. You feel the coffee, too hot, and then later, too cold. The in-between is not experienced as duration until afterward, when it is given one.
