a field guide to internal states

Collected from the field. Descriptions accurate at the time of collection. All specimens observed in their natural habitat. No specimens were harmed.

001

Sunday Evening Melancholy

Melankholia dominicalis

Family Temporalis (time-adjacent feelings) Habitat Late Sunday afternoons, particularly in autumn; found near windows or in rooms where the light is changing Behavior Arrives quietly around 4pm; intensifies toward 6pm; produces a sense that the week is beginning again without sufficient preparation; occasionally generates a desire to reorganize small things Distinguishing features Distinct from ordinary sadness by its temporal specificity; does not persist past Monday morning; highly predictable; responds poorly to productivity Conservation status Abundant; perhaps the most common of the temporal-affective species
002

Pre-Sleep Clarity

Claritas nocturna

Family Cogitandia (late-arising insights) Habitat The period between lying down and falling asleep; exclusively nocturnal; disappears in daylight Behavior Produces sudden solutions to problems the waking mind could not solve; accompanied by the certainty that everything will be fine; evaporates if the host attempts to record it Distinguishing features Characterized by a spurious lucidity; most specimens are convinced, in the moment, that they will remember this in the morning Conservation status Common; annual loss of insights estimated as incalculable
003

Retroactive Embarrassment

Erubescentia retrocognita

Family Memorialis (memory-adjacent conditions) Habitat Commutes, showers, transitional spaces, the moment before sleep Behavior Activates without warning; replays a specific social failure from the recent or distant past; the original event may be years old but is presented with urgency; shows no preference for significant failures over trivial ones Distinguishing features Notably democratic — a mispronunciation from 2009 receives the same urgency as a genuine error; duration: 30 seconds to four minutes Conservation status Invasive; immune to reason; thrives without maintenance
004

Anticipatory Nostalgia

Nostalgia praecox

Family Temporalis (time-adjacent feelings) Habitat Moments of happiness; vacation endings; last days; watching someone fall asleep Behavior Generates grief about the ending of an experience during the experience itself; first documented in diaries, then photographs; may intensify happiness while partially removing the subject from it Distinguishing features The only species that coexists with its opposite; simultaneously a symptom of caring deeply; not harmful in small doses Conservation status May be increasing; linked to conditions of impermanence
005

The 2am Certainty

Certitudo vespertina

Family Cogitandia (late-arising insights) Habitat 2–4am; periods of insomnia; brief waking Behavior Produces absolute conviction about a course of action that will seem less clear at 9am; subjects report that "this time it's different"; the certainty does not survive breakfast; related to Claritas nocturna but more confident and less accurate Distinguishing features Characterized by urgency out of proportion to consequences; may produce notes, messages, or plans that are cryptic in the morning Conservation status Persistent despite poor outcomes
006

The Unlocatable Feeling

Affectus nescio

Family Unnamed (resists classification) Habitat Ordinary moments; arrives mid-task — washing dishes, walking somewhere familiar Behavior A distinct emotional state without identifiable cause; the host is aware of feeling something but cannot identify what or why; does not respond to naming; typically persists 5–40 minutes; departs without trace Distinguishing features May be the default state that language conceals, surfacing briefly when language is temporarily suspended Conservation status Unknown — possibly the most common of all states, and simply the least documented

All specimens are native to the interior. Descriptions may vary between hosts. The classification system is provisional.
