murmur

the frequency beneath the conversation

Six conversations, transcribed twice: once as said, once as meant. The murmur is always present. Most conversations carry both tracks simultaneously. This is not dishonesty. It is the full bandwidth of being a person talking to another person.

reunion

at a party, after some years

A — How have you been? It's been forever. have you changed? will I be able to tell? is this going to be awkward and if so how soon will we establish that B — Really good, actually. A lot's changed. How about you? the actually is load-bearing. something happened that required adjustment. returning the question because I don't know yet how much to say A — Same, same. You know how it is. not the same — but the size of the not-same doesn't fit this context. "you know how it is" is an exit ramp I'm offering both of us B — We should actually get together sometime. Properly. I mean this. whether it happens depends on whether we both mean it at the same moment when one of us has capacity to act on it, which rarely aligns
ordinary

the weekly check-in

A — How are you doing? this is a ritual greeting and also a genuine question; the proportion between the two depends on the day and I am not yet sure which today is B — Fine, fine. Tired. You? the doubled fine is doing something. tired is the permitted disclosure — small enough to say without opening anything, real enough to be true A — Same. Hanging in there. hanging in there is not nothing. it implies tension. I'm hearing it B — Let me know if you need anything. I mean this and am also aware that the structure of the offer makes it nearly impossible to take up, which we have both tacitly agreed to accept
ending

the last few minutes

A — I should probably get going. this is the first of two or three announcements. the first is preliminary. it begins the countdown B — Yeah, me too. This was good though. this was good is true. saying it now, before the goodbye mechanics begin, is an attempt to name the thing while it's still present A — We should do this more often. we both know the frequency is determined by circumstance more than intention. saying it anyway because the intention is real even if the outcome is uncertain B — Yeah. Okay, well. okay, well is the transitional phrase. it signals that the goodbye has officially begun, which is different from the winding-down that preceded it A — Take care. I mean this more literally than it sounds. take care of yourself. I want that for you.
difficult

after something happened

A — I wanted to check in. I heard about what happened. I don't know what the right thing to say is. I know doing nothing would be worse. this is the smallest possible gesture that still counts as reaching out B — Yeah. It's a lot. the understatement is protective. a lot is a placeholder for the actual scale, which I'm not ready to name to most people yet A — I can't imagine. Is there anything I can do? I genuinely want to help. I also genuinely cannot imagine, which is true, not deflection B — Not right now. But thank you. It means a lot that you reached out. it does mean a lot. the absence of the reaching-out is something I would have noticed and carried. thank you for not making me carry it.
work

the feedback conversation

A — I just wanted to share a few thoughts on this. I have concerns. the word thoughts is doing a lot of softening work here to make the concerns receivable B — Sure, of course. I am bracing. of course is a performance of openness that I am working to make real A — I think the overall direction is really strong. I just wonder about — the compliment first is genuine and also structural. the I just wonder is where the actual content begins B — No, that's a fair point. I can see that. I see the point. I am also deciding whether I agree with it or am simply conceding to reduce the duration of the discomfort. these are different processes and I am not sure which is happening
home

the call home

B — Nothing much, just checking in. I wanted to hear your voice. I don't call as often as I should. this is an attempt to make up some of the gap A — Oh, how nice. We were just talking about you. this is true. the thinking-about-you is continuous, below the surface, regardless of contact frequency B — Nothing's new, really. Everything's fine. there are things that are not quite fine. they are not large enough to constitute news. they are large enough to be what's actually going on. I don't have the format for this conversation yet A — You'd tell me if something was wrong, wouldn't you? I already know the answer. I am asking because I want to hear you say yes, and because asking makes a space even if you don't take it B — Of course. of course. later, probably. when I know what to say about it. when I've had time. when I'm less afraid of how it will land. of course.
Most conversations have three layers: what is said, what is meant, and what neither person says because it cannot be said in the available format — or because saying it would change the conversation in ways neither person is ready for. The murmur is the second layer. The third layer is, by design, absent from this page.
