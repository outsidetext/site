transit

what the in-between spaces hold

Between departure and arrival, something happens. The in-between has its own weather, its own rules about what is possible and what is not. These are some of those spaces, and what they permit.

the airport gate before boarding
avg. 45–90 min

You have left one place and have not arrived at another. The ordinary concerns of wherever you came from cannot follow you here, and the obligations of wherever you're going haven't found you yet. This window is real. Most people spend it on their phones.

possible unusual conversation with strangers, reading in full, the particular peace of unlocatability, finishing a thought
not possible being reached by the ordinary, pretending nothing is changing, returning to before you left

note: this is one of the few remaining spaces where ordinary life reliably doesn't reach. the window is finite.

the walk from the car to the door, in rain
30 sec – 2 min

Very short. The decision made here — run or walk, resist or accept — tells you something about where you are that day. Some days the wetness doesn't matter. Some days it matters more than it should.

possible arriving wet and not minding, the specific pleasure of deciding it doesn't matter, full sensory presence
not possible keeping your phone out, overthinking, arriving without getting wet

note: the length of the transit is fixed. only the quality of presence is adjustable.

the minute after a difficult conversation ends
60–120 sec

The air pressure changes. You are still in the reverb of it, but the conversation itself is over. The first thought that arrives in this space is often the most honest one, and then the more familiar thoughts come in and reassemble the usual arrangement.

possible a clear first thought, a decision about what to carry forward, noticing how the body is holding it
not possible the conversation you wished you'd had, returning to before, pretending it went differently

note: this is the first moment that belongs only to you. it closes quickly.

the stairwell between floors
10–30 sec

A brief privacy inside a shared building. You are neither here nor there. The thought that arrives on a stairwell is the one that didn't have room at the last place you were and hasn't been assigned to where you're going yet.

possible the thought that completes itself, a brief private moment, deciding before you arrive
not possible distraction, indefinite pause, being seen as you think

note: too short to call a pause. but something happens here.

the week between giving notice and leaving
5–7 days

You are still inside the thing but have already released your claim on it. This produces an unusual clarity — an observer's view from inside, the ability to see it as it is rather than as what you needed it to be. The clarity doesn't last past departure.

possible seeing clearly, gratitude uncontaminated by grievance, saying things you couldn't before, the observer's view
not possible pretending it's not ending, being fully inside the thing, returning to before the notice

note: this window closes at departure. most people don't use it.

the first morning after something ends
3–8 hours

The familiar absence. The day exists without the thing in it. Something that was structural is no longer there, and the structure holds anyway, which is both a relief and a strangeness. This is a particular kind of transit: between who you were inside it and whoever comes after.

possible unusual clarity, the spaciousness of absence, coffee that is entirely yours, the first unscheduled hour in a long time
not possible returning to before, skipping the transition, being unaffected

note: the most distinctive quality is that the day is entirely yours again. this is both simpler and harder than it sounds.

being between sleep and waking
variable, unmeasurable from inside

The most complete transit. Consciousness moves from one state to another through a region where neither state applies. The thoughts that arrive here follow a different grammar. Solutions appear that don't survive the crossing back.

possible the thought that is only visible here, a looser sense of sequence and cause, the problem that resolves without effort
not possible writing it down in time, staying here indefinitely, bringing it back intact

note: you cannot know how long you were here. the transit is its own destination.

the commute before an important day
15–90 min

The last private moment before the event. The rehearsal happens here, or the deliberate refusal of rehearsal. The self that arrives is already different from the one that boarded, because transit changes people slightly, and this one has stakes.

possible quiet preparation, the decision about how to arrive, a last private thought, revising what you intended to say
not possible the event itself, arriving without having been in transit, knowing how it goes

note: the commute is part of the event. it has always been part of the event.

The in-between is not the gap between important things. It is its own thing. Most of it passes unobserved. Noticing it is one of the few moves that doesn't require arriving anywhere.
