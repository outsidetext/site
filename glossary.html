glossary

words for things that needed naming

screenache
n.
the fatigue that settles behind the eyes after long hours in front of light; a tiredness that sleep doesn't quite reach, because it isn't tiredness exactly — it's the feeling of having been somewhere without going anywhere
tab guilt
n.
the quiet, persistent shame of thirty-seven browser tabs held open in good faith — each one a small promise made to a former self who intended to read, to research, to follow up; the paralysis that forms when intentions accumulate faster than time
404 grief
n.
the particular small loss of arriving at a URL and finding nothing there; the internet's form of forgetting, which is absolute and unannounced — no funeral, no forwarding address, just the page that was once a place
ghost scroll
n.
the reflex of pulling down at the bottom of a feed, looking for content that isn't there; a hunger the thumb learned without consent, persisting even when the phone is dark, even when the hand is doing something else
pre-nostalgia
n.
the ache of missing something while still inside it; the grief that arrives before the loss, so that you spend the good moment also mourning it — at dinner, on a walk, near the end of a good book
signal lag
n.
the three seconds between sending a message and wondering if you should have; the gap where regret is born, where the meaning of words begins to shift slightly, where everything you didn't say becomes loud
the draft
n.
the version of something written and not sent; the honest letter, the clarifying response, the thing that needed saying — living in a folder, or a drawer, or the space between thinking and speaking, where most true things reside permanently
deep tab
n.
a browser tab opened months ago and never closed — carrying the weight of something once urgent, now forgotten, too old to act on but too charged to dismiss; a small monument to a past version of what mattered
cursor drift
n.
the way a mouse moves toward the corner of a screen when attention wanders; the body's honest report on where the mind has gone; a small, involuntary confession
night mode
n.
not a display setting, but the version of yourself that appears after ten p.m. — when the day's performance ends, the filters come down, and you become, briefly, more accurate; the self that stays up too late because returning to sleep feels like agreeing to start over
