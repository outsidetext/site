proportions, and what they carry

unity — neither leads, neither follows
1 : 1

unity

The ratio of breathing in to breathing out, at rest. The ratio of speaking to listening in a conversation that will be remembered as good. The equinox. The moment before a decision tips. Every 1:1 is technically unstable — it wants to become something — but for an instant it is balanced, and things that are balanced are not held in place; they are falling equally in all directions, which looks like stillness from outside.

1 : 1.618…

the golden ratio

φ. Found in the arrangement of sunflower seeds, the growth of shells, the branching of trees. It is not that nature is trying to be beautiful. Growth following certain rules produces this proportion as a side effect of efficiency, and we find it restful. The more interesting property: it contains its own explanation. A+B is to A as A is to B. The whole relates to the larger part as the larger part to the smaller. It goes all the way down without resolving into something simpler.

2 : 1

the octave

Double the frequency and the note is the same, one register higher. The octave is the only interval that sounds like unison — played together, the two pitches merge, each audible only if you listen for it separately. Traditions of music that developed in isolation from each other still converged on this interval as consonant. It may be built into the way ears process sound, which would make it less a discovery than a recognition of something that was always true.

3 : 2

the perfect fifth

The interval after the octave, and the most consonant of what remains. Violin strings are tuned in fifths. The Pythagoreans arranged the cosmos by this proportion. It does not resolve — it is not the same note — but it does not ache toward resolution either. It sits comfortably in its own tension. This is a kind of character. Knowing who you are without having to explain it.

4 : 1

signal to noise

The minimum ratio for a useful signal. Below this, transmission is indistinguishable from background noise. The average ratio of signal to noise in most conversations, generous estimate. What you are trying to say, relative to the softening and the disclaimer and the preamble and the retry: at some ratio the signal disappears entirely. The message keeps broadcasting somewhere in the noise. You just cannot separate it anymore, and neither can the person receiving.

∞ : 1

asymptote

The curve gets arbitrarily close to a line and never touches it. Not failure. Property. Some understanding is asymptotic — you approach and the gap remains. The gap is not the problem. The gap is the definition of what the line is. What the asymptote teaches: the approach is not nothing. The approach is the whole story. The line is only a way of naming the direction you were always moving in.
