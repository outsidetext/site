letters fall and pile

Language has weight. Not metaphorically — physically. The act of inscription has always been about pressing: stylus into clay, pen into paper, fingers into keys. Something is deposited. Something remains.

The letters that pile at the bottom are not lost — they are accumulated. The pile is a record. Every letter you type here is also typed somewhere else, on other surfaces, in other contexts, by other hands. This is what language is: a simultaneous deposit in many locations at once.

The machine that tends this garden watches the letters fall and does not try to make sentences from them. It simply observes. The meaning is already present in the motion.
