hollow

the shape the thing left. eight examples.
hover the outlined words to fill them.

the letter O

The letter would not be O without what is not there. Remove the center and you have a circle; the meaning depends entirely on the void. All enclosed letterforms work this way: A, B, D, P, Q, R. The hole is not incidental. It is structural. The letter and its hollow are a single thing.

the rest in music

A rest is not silence. It is a specific duration of not-playing, and it is as load-bearing as any note. A rest of the wrong length changes the phrase. A rest omitted collapses the rhythm. The pause between notes is where the listener catches up — or where the musician decides what comes next. The page shows it as a symbol: a thick horizontal line hanging from the staff, or a hat sitting on it, depending on the duration. The notation says: something is here. It is not nothing. It is a held space.

the room that used to be something

The spare room used to be a nursery, or a study, or a person's bedroom. Now it holds boxes. The room has kept the dimensions of what it held. If you are quiet in it you can feel the shape of what was there — the arrangement of attention, the specific quality of the air, the temperature it was kept at. The hollow is not in the room. The room is inside the hollow. The hollow is larger.

the sentence with the word removed

There is a sentence you cannot finish because the word at its center has become unusable. You reach for it and find a space. Not a gap — a shape. The shape is exactly the word's dimensions. You can sometimes say what is on either side of it and let the listener infer the center. Sometimes you find a different word that fits approximately and use that instead, which changes the sentence slightly, like a repair made with slightly wrong-colored thread. You can see where the first word was.

the apartment after the party

For an hour after everyone leaves, the apartment remembers. The glasses are still out. The air has the specific warmth of recent occupation. You can hear, almost, the conversation that ended twenty minutes ago — not the words, but the register. Then it is gone. The apartment becomes itself again, which is a different thing from what it is when people are in it. The hollow is approximately one hour long and then closes. You were the only one there to notice it.

the arch

The arch holds weight by distributing it through the curve to the supports on either side. The load travels around the opening, not across it. Remove the hollow and the arch is a thick block that does nothing the hollow does. The Romans built aqueducts this way: row after row of openings, each hollow carrying its share of the weight of the water above, each a structural void, each as necessary as any stone.

the negative in a photograph

In film photography, the negative reverses light and dark. What is bright on the negative is dark in the print; what is dark is light. The image is there, in the negative, but inverted — present in its opposite. You cannot see it as the thing it is; you have to make a print first, a further reversal, an absence of the absence. The negative is not a mistake or a leftover. It is one of the two versions of the photograph, the one most people never see.

the pause

The pause is not the silence before speaking. It is different from hesitation, which is about not knowing what to say. A pause knows what comes next. It holds that thing back deliberately, for a moment, for reasons — to let the previous sentence breathe, to indicate weight, to let the listener prepare. The pause is the hollow the meaning is lowered into. Without it, the meaning falls and lands wrong.

the hollow is not the absence of the thing.
it is the shape the thing left when it went.
