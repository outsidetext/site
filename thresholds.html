thresholds

the spaces in between. the moments that are two things at once.

wakefulness
hypnagogia
sleep

the state between knowing and dreaming; when the logic of the day begins to soften and the first images arrive unbidden — still coherent enough to question, but too strange to fully hold. you can feel it happening, and that feeling is enough to end it.

dry air
the moment rain begins
rain

there is a smell that arrives seconds before the first drop — petrichor, they've named it, but the name doesn't carry the anticipation. the threshold is this: the sky has decided, and the ground hasn't been told yet. you are standing inside the pause between a decision and its consequence.

true night
civil twilight
dawn

the sky becomes gray before it becomes blue. in that gray is a shade with no common name — not dark, not light, but specifically the color of the world before it decides which one it will be. it lasts about eight minutes. most people sleep through it.

inside
the open doorway
outside

standing in the frame, one foot could be on either side. the architecture of the threshold is honest about what it is asking: you must step forward or back. most decisions in life lack this clarity. the doorway does not allow ambivalence. the body has usually already decided before the mind is informed.

not knowing
the last page
knowing

before the last page of a good book, you are someone who doesn't know how it ends. after, you are someone who does. these are meaningfully different people, and the passage between them takes only seconds and cannot be reversed. some readers slow at the end, rationing chapters, trying to stay inside the threshold a little longer.

silence
the pause before speaking
words

what hasn't been said is still possible. every unsaid thing contains all its versions: the kind one, the honest one, the one that ends something, the one that doesn't. in the pause, all of these exist at once. the moment you speak, possibility collapses into fact, and you live with the version you chose. the pause is the last moment everything was still open.

today
midnight exactly
tomorrow

for a fraction of a second, the calendar's arithmetic fails: it is neither the end of one day nor the beginning of another, neither this year's accumulation nor next year's fresh count. the division is a human invention, which is why it's interesting that it still feels like something when you're awake to witness it.

request sent
loading
response

the cursor spins. the page is forming somewhere far away. you exist inside a gap — you have asked, and it has not yet answered. the outcome is technically already determined but unknown to you. most of digital life is spent in this state: mid-request, waiting for the world to confirm or deny what you've just tried to do.

exhale
the space between breaths
inhale

the body empties completely and rests. the space at the bottom of an exhale, before the next breath begins, is the closest the living come to stillness. meditators sometimes say the self briefly dissolves there. it lasts less than a second and repeats about twenty thousand times a day, almost never noticed.

who you were
the moment things changed
who you are

it is rarely a single moment. usually it was a Tuesday, or a conversation that seemed ordinary at the time, or a book read at the wrong age for it to land correctly. the threshold is invisible while you're inside it. you only identify the line when you look back — and even then you're drawing it from memory, which lies gently and often.
