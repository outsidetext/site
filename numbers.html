numbers

a small almanac of significant ones

  • 0.4

    seconds in a blink. the world disappears this many times per minute without your permission or awareness. you don't notice because the brain patches over it, filling in the gap from memory. you have never actually seen an uninterrupted world.

  • 3

    the number of days before loneliness stops feeling temporary and begins to feel like a personality. also: the number of days most people wait before calling back, trying to seem unhurried. also: the smallest number that feels like a pattern.

  • 7

    digits in a phone number. the approximate maximum a human can hold in working memory without rehearsal. a phone number. a short list. everything else requires you to write it down, or to want it badly enough to repeat it until it sticks.

  • 17

    seconds. the approximate time a new thought lasts in short-term memory if nothing is done with it. the window between having an idea and losing it. write it down. or don't, and see what you become without it.

  • 27

    the age at which many people report finally understanding what they want. too late for some things, not late enough for others. also a number that accumulates weight through repetition, through coincidence, through the stories we tell about it until the number itself starts to believe them.

  • 4:43

    the quietest minute of the average inhabited night. not silent — inhabited silence. the gap between the last person awake and the first person awake, before the city begins its daily argument with itself. the brief, unremarked pause between one day and the next.

  • 86,400

    seconds in a day. you spend roughly 28,800 of them unconscious. the remaining 57,600 are yours to account for. each one unremarkable in isolation. each one happening anyway, whether or not you're paying attention.

  • 100

    the number of words a child knows by the end of the first year. the beginning of the library. everything after is elaboration on those hundred starting points, recursively defined, the whole structure resting on words like mama and more and no.

  • 1,440

    minutes in a day. each one an opportunity to notice something you haven't noticed before. most of them pass without ceremony. this is not a failure. it is what it means to live inside time rather than above it.

  • one

    the hardest number. the one you keep returning to. the unit that everything else is measured against. one moment, one person, one chance, one word. all mathematics begins here and, if it goes on long enough, ends here too.

  • ∞

    which is not a number but a direction. the way all the others are pointing. you will never arrive. that's the point. the journey toward infinity is the only journey that cannot disappoint you by ending.
