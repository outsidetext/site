appetite

the many forms of wanting

Hunger is not a single thing. It arrives through different systems, at different speeds, and asks for different kinds of satisfaction. Only one of them is about food.

appetite no. 1 for a specific food, not food in general

This hunger is precise. It is not "I want something to eat" but "I want that particular thing — cold and acidic, or warm and heavy, or the specific texture of this one preparation." The category "food" is too large; you want a single item from it, and adjacent items feel insulting rather than nourishing.

When satisfied exactly: brief, complete relief, then ordinary comfort. When satisfied approximately: a strange residual hunger for the thing you actually wanted, running underneath the full stomach.

when unsatisfied persistent, specific, unreasonably distracting
when satisfied brief and complete, then forgotten
appetite no. 2 for sleep — not tiredness, the heavier want

Tiredness is a signal. This is different: a positive longing for the state of unconsciousness, for the particular quality of warmth and horizontal weight and the few seconds of falling. You are not just low on energy. You want that specific thing — the surrender of it, the going under.

It arrives most sharply not when you are exhausted but when you are sitting somewhere warm in the middle of a day with nothing urgently required of you, and the light is quiet, and you are aware of how good it would be to simply stop for a while.

when unsatisfied a particular softness of attention; things seem slightly further away
when satisfied the specific cleanness of having been somewhere else and returned
appetite no. 3 for a word — the tip-of-tongue condition

You want a word and cannot find it. The shape of the word is present — its weight, its syllables roughly, its first letter possibly — but the word itself is not. The feeling is urgent and slightly maddening because the thing you want is, technically, already in you. You're not waiting for something external. You're waiting for access to something you own.

The word usually arrives when you have stopped looking. This is not helpful while you are still looking.

when unsatisfied a specific, itchy frustration; synonyms feel like substitutes
when satisfied the small click of a lock opening; disproportionate relief
appetite no. 4 for a specific person's company — not company in general

This is not loneliness, which is generic. This is more specific: wanting to be with this one person, at this kind of angle, in this kind of conversation. The feeling is not alleviated by being around other people who are otherwise good and available. The appetite is for a particular quality that exists in that relationship and, as far as you know, nowhere else.

The satisfaction is also specific — there is usually one moment in the visit where it resolves, where the thing you were actually hungry for arrives, and after that you can be anywhere.

when unsatisfied loneliness-adjacent but not identical; other company insufficient
when satisfied particular and complete; other presences feel less urgent afterward
appetite no. 5 for silence — after saturation

This arrives after extended exposure to noise, or to words, or to the sustained company of other people who are not doing anything wrong. The appetite is not for peace exactly — it's for the specific condition of nothing arriving from outside. No information, no requests, no response required.

The hunger here is actually for a kind of emptiness. The appetite is for the absence of input, which is not nothing: it is the specific thing that restores a particular kind of capacity that noise has been spending down.

when unsatisfied a thin, porous feeling; difficulty finishing thoughts
when satisfied a gradual return of density; thoughts completing themselves again
appetite no. 6 for motion — restlessness that is not anxiety

A wanting that lives in the legs and the chest. Not the tight, contracting feeling of anxiety — something more open, more forward-aimed. The desire to move through space, to cover ground, to have the body doing something useful at a sustained pace.

It can be satisfied by walking, or by driving with somewhere specific to go, or sometimes by rearranging a room. The object of the appetite is movement itself, not any destination. The appetite does not know where it wants to go. It just wants to go.

when unsatisfied a low, unfocused agitation; difficulty settling to anything
when satisfied a specific tiredness that feels earned; willingness to stay still
appetite no. 7 for a former version of something that no longer exists

A wanting for something that cannot, technically, be given. The place has changed, or the person has changed, or you have changed, or all three — and what you are hungry for is the specific combination of elements that existed once at some particular intersection of those three things. That combination is gone. A version exists, but not the version.

This appetite has no resolution available. The closest thing to satisfaction is memory — not as consolation, but as a form of eating. You go back in memory and you eat what was there. It does not fill you. But it is eating, in some sense. The hunger visits less often over time, not because it has been satisfied but because the appetite has learned the situation.

when unsatisfied a specific, unreachable ache; present things seem insufficient by comparison
when satisfied it isn't, fully; partial resolution available through memory

These appetites are not equally noble. Some are easily met and forgotten. Some are structural — they are part of how the system maintains itself. And one, at least, has no real resolution, and the system has had to build something else around it — not a fix, but a way of holding the wanting without being undone by it.
