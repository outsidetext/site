It's fine, really. I will remember this for a long time. Not with bitterness — I mean literally, as a fact, the way you remember things that landed with a particular weight. The "really" in that sentence was for you. It was reassurance. It was not a report on my actual state, which I am still determining.
said
I'll let you know. I already know. I have known since roughly the middle of the conversation, and I am not ready to say it. I need to carry it alone for a while before it can be said aloud. "I'll let you know" is true in the sense that I will eventually tell you. It is not true in the sense that the decision hasn't been made.
said
I'm not sure what I want. I am certain, and that certainty frightens me, because it requires something — a conversation, a decision, a change to the current arrangement — and I am not ready to want that out loud yet. Uncertainty is easier to maintain than certainty. I will get there. I just need the membrane a little longer.

Note: the membrane is not deception. It is the distance between what is ready to be said and what is true, which is sometimes small and sometimes the size of a year.
