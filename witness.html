what witnessing costs

To witness something is to make it real in the sense of having occurred — to provide a record, however impermanent, however internal. The witness is changed by the witnessing in ways that outlast the memory of the thing witnessed. You cannot unwitness. This is why testimony is considered valuable and why presence at an event carries weight that hearing about it secondhand does not. The witness changes the event too, slightly, by being there. The observed and the observer are in a system. Neither exits unchanged.

the unwatched thing

A tree falling in an empty forest does not go unwitnessed. The other trees feel the pressure wave through their root networks; they register a change in neighboring activity and respond, slowly, over seasons. The microbes in the soil receive the impact. The displacement of air propagates outward as a sound wave even if no eardrum is present to transduce it. Witnessing is not exclusive to consciousness. The universe witnesses everything, continuously, without interpretation. The question is not whether the event was witnessed but whether it was interpreted — whether a mind was present to assign it meaning and carry that meaning forward.

to be seen accurately

Rarer than to be seen. To be seen accurately requires that the observer have no stake in the outcome, no theory to confirm, no prior image to match the current one against. Most of what passes for observation is confirmation: we see what we expected to see, what we needed to see, what our previous encounters trained us to see. Accurate witnessing is close to love in its requirements if not its consequences — it demands that you put the object ahead of what you wanted the object to be. This is harder than it sounds. Most of us manage it for about thirty seconds before the interpretation begins.

the self as witness

You are watching yourself read this sentence. The part of you that watches has always been there and cannot itself be the thing being watched — to observe it is to step back into a further watching, which can also be observed, and so on. You cannot get all the way out. This is not a problem to be solved. It is the structure of consciousness, which is not a thing but a relation: always a watching of something, always a watching of the watching. The witness is not separate from the witnessed self; it is another fold of the same thing, turned back on itself, which is what thinking is.
