odds

estimated probabilities of small emotional events

methodology: informal. sample size: one life, approximately. margin of error: considerable. these figures are revised whenever something surprises us.

  • a day that asks nothing of you 1 in 12 they come. you don't always notice until they're almost over.
  • the song that just came on being exactly the right one varies improves significantly after dark. on long drives: much higher. in waiting rooms: approaches zero.
  • waking up and knowing immediately what day it is 3 in 7 better on weekdays, when the day announces itself by weight. weekends are harder to tell apart.
  • saying exactly what you meant 1 in 4 higher when writing than speaking. highest at 2am. lowest mid-argument.
  • recognising a place you have never been uncommon not impossible. occurs most often in cities that resemble other cities, or in light that falls a certain way.
  • the feeling of almost remembering something important daily a specific kind of forgetting. the thing being almost-remembered is often not as important as the feeling of almost-remembering it.
  • a conversation going the way you hoped it would less than you'd think but more than you'd fear. the gap is where most conversations actually live.
  • a message arriving at exactly the right moment rare, but notable you remember these disproportionately. they feel like design, but they're probably luck.
  • forgetting a name mid-sentence, with the face entirely clear 1 in 8 encounters increases with age, or with the number of names you've accumulated. the face always remains. the name usually returns within four minutes.
  • noticing you are happy, while you are still happy 1 in 30 days usually the recognition is retrospective. the moments arrive whole but are named only afterward.
  • finishing something difficult to estimate the unfinished ones outnumber. but the finished ones tend to matter more than their count suggests.
  • a dream remembered fully at noon 1 in 40 fragments survive more often. the emotional tone survives longest. sometimes only a colour remains by evening.

these figures are not reliable. they are, however, considered.
