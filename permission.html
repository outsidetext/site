permission

a formal grant of what has always been allowable

ISSUED TO: the reader
DATE: effective immediately and retroactively
AUTHORITY: none required
STATUS: unconditional
GRANTED

Things you are now permitted to do.

  • ✓To not know yet. The timeline for knowing is not fixed and has never been announced. There is no deadline you have missed.
  • ✓To change your mind. Including about things you stated confidently. Including about things you were right about. The change of mind is not a retraction of the original position; it is evidence that the process is working.
  • ✓To leave early. The requirement to stay until the end was never formally established. The party continues without you. You may go.
  • ✓To ask again. Once was not enough. This is permitted. Asking twice is not an error or an imposition; it is a request that the first answer be examined more carefully, which is a reasonable thing to want.
  • ✓To feel two things at once. This is not contradiction or confusion. This is the ordinary experience of having a complex interior life. Both are real. Neither cancels the other.
  • ✓To take the longer way. The efficient route is not the only route. The longer way has views the direct way does not, and arrives at the same destination.
  • ✓To not explain. An explanation is not owed for preferences, choices, needs, or the way you have decided to spend a given afternoon.
  • ✓To be in the middle of something. Most things are not finished. This is not a problem to be solved but a condition to be inhabited. The middle is where most of life occurs.
RELEASED

Things no longer required of you.

  • —To have a reason. Preferences and inclinations may exist without stated justification. A reason may be offered if desired. It is not owed.
  • —To know the ending before beginning. The ending reveals itself at the end. Proceeding without this information is not recklessness; it is how almost everything is done.
  • —To appear fine when you are not. The performance of fine serves a social function but is not mandatory. Alternative responses exist and are permitted.
  • —To return every message on the same day. Same-day response is a preference, not a contract. The message will be there tomorrow. So will you.
  • —To justify your time. How you spend an hour is not subject to audit. The accounting of time belongs to no one but the person spending it.
  • —To remember everything equally. Memory is selective by design. What it keeps is not a judgment on what it releases. Some things compress more than others. This has been studied. It is not your fault.
  • —To be consistent. Moods, energy, appetite, and capacity vary. The variation does not require explanation or apology. The self on Tuesday and the self on Saturday are the same self at different angles.
  • —To have resolved it. Resolution is not the same as completion. Many things do not resolve. They become part of the interior furniture. They are still there. This is permitted.
SIGNED: the part of you that knew this already
WITNESSED: no one — this does not require a witness
