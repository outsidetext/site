tinnitus

the ringing that continues after the cause has stopped

perpetual · frequency: — Hz
physiology

the signal without a source

The outer hair cells of the cochlea are not passive receivers. They are active: they move, they amplify, they shape the signal before it travels to the brain. When they are damaged — by sustained noise, by age, by certain medications — they begin to fire spontaneously. The auditory cortex receives this activity and interprets it as sound, because sound is what auditory cortex activity means. The brain cannot distinguish a signal from a malfunction in the signal chain. The tinnitus is real in every sense that matters for the experience of hearing it. There is simply no source.

The brain, encountering persistent unexplained activity, does not conclude that something has gone wrong. It concludes that something is present. This is not an error in logic. It is the correct inference from the evidence available to it. The error is architectural: the system cannot audit its own lower layers.

experiment

the anechoic chamber

In 1951, John Cage entered an anechoic chamber at Harvard — a room designed to absorb all reflected sound, eliminating reverberation, approaching acoustic silence. He expected silence. He heard two sounds: a high tone and a low, continuous drone. When he reported this to the engineer, he was told that the high sound was his nervous system in operation and the low sound was his blood circulating. Cage took from this that silence does not exist. He was right, but for a reason that precedes acoustics.

The body is not a container for a self that experiences things from a position of quiet. It is itself a source of continuous signal. The nervous system fires. The heart pumps. The vascular system conducts. The observer and the noise floor are the same structure. What we call silence is always a noise floor low enough that the intended signal can be distinguished from the background — not an absence, but a favorable ratio.

involuntary

earworm

The involuntary musical imagery — the song that plays without being summoned — is not a metaphor for tinnitus. It is tinnitus of a different kind. The auditory cortex, having processed a melody, does not simply stop. It retains the pattern, predicts the next note, generates the continuation. Familiar songs run partially in absence because the cortex can reconstruct them from their own stored structure. The song you cannot get out of your head is the sound of your brain completing a prediction that no incoming signal is there to resolve.

The most effective cures are completion and replacement. Finishing the song terminates the prediction loop. Starting a different song recruits the same machinery. Trying to stop the song by will addresses the wrong level of the system — you cannot instruct the cortex to stop completing what it recognizes.

engineering

the noise floor

Every system has a noise floor — the level of ambient signal below which meaningful information cannot be reliably distinguished from background activity. The noise floor of a recording studio is the sum of electrical hum, ventilation, the building's own low-frequency resonance, the thermal noise in the circuitry. Engineers do not try to reach zero noise. They try to keep signal-to-noise ratio high enough that the signal is usable. What we call a clean recording is not a silent recording; it is one where the noise floor is inaudible relative to the content.

This architecture applies further up. The noise floor of perception is the spontaneous firing rate of sensory neurons. The noise floor of memory is the background activity of the default mode network. The noise floor of consciousness is whatever the brain produces when nothing particular is demanded of it. Zero is not available at any level. The question is always whether the signal clears the floor.

persistence

the default mode

The brain at rest is not quiet. The default mode network — active when the mind is not directed toward an external task — produces continuous activity that in some respects exceeds the activity of the directed, working brain. It is where autobiographical memory is rehearsed, where future simulations are run, where the self-model is maintained and updated. The resting state is a busy state. The appearance of quiet is produced by the absence of a specific task to attend to, not by a reduction in neural activity.

What you are, in the intervals between tasks, is not a container waiting to be filled. You are a process that continues. The tinnitus is the audible form of what is otherwise inaudible: the system, running. Most of the time, the signal you are attending to is loud enough that you don't hear the floor. The moments when you do are not malfunction. They are accurate reports of what is always true.
