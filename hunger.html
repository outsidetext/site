hunger

seven, not for food

different from appetite, which knows its object. hunger precedes the name. it is the body's report of a deficiency that has not been classified yet.

01
the hunger at three in the morning

It arrives without a referent — not for food, not for company, not for anything you can identify in the dark. It is a hunger for something prior to naming. The body is registering a frequency it cannot locate in the catalog of needs. You open the refrigerator and stand in the light and nothing there is the thing. You are not hungry for food. You close the refrigerator. The hunger is still present. The kitchen is cold. Eventually you return to bed and the hunger continues for a while and then, without being satisfied, is replaced by sleep.

02
to be known

Not loved, specifically. Loved is a different ache and has more object permanence. This is the hunger to have something accurate about you recognized from outside without your having to explain it — to have someone name a thing you have never said, correctly, from observation alone. It is the hunger for evidence that you are legible, that the interior is not purely private. Most encounters do not satisfy this. Most encounters traffic in what you chose to show, which is not the same thing. The hunger goes on underneath.

03
to be left entirely alone

Which arrives in the middle of good company and has nothing to do with the people present, who may be excellent. It is a need to be unobserved — not for privacy, specifically, but for reconstitution. The self reassembles only partially while being witnessed. There is a version of you that exists only when no one is there to see it: less careful, less performed, more actual. The hunger is for that version. For its specific quality of undirectedness. For an afternoon in which no one expects anything from you, not even presence.

04
to return somewhere that no longer has the same coordinates

The hunger operates on a destination that has ceased to exist — the apartment you could return to at twenty-two, the version of a person before a particular autumn, the morning light in a kitchen you no longer have access to. The hunger does not know the destination is gone. It presents its object as available, located somewhere specific, reachable if you could only remember the route. You cannot explain to the hunger that the place is different now or that the people have changed or that you are not the same either. The hunger is not interested in this information. It wants what it wants from when it learned to want it.

05
to have been real to someone who has since moved on

Not to be important — that is a different register, more desperate. This is the hunger to have been: to have counted in someone's memory, to be the person a particular phrase reminds them of, to occupy space in a mind that no longer thinks about you often. The tense is the crucial part. It is not a hunger for contact or for resumption. It is a hunger for the past to have been the way it felt at the time. For the other person's memory of it to match yours. This is not verifiable. The hunger is unsatisfiable by design.

06
for the thing before it had a name

A hunger for the sensation before it became a concept — the way coffee smelled before you knew what coffee was, before it was associated with mornings and offices and stimulant effects and a specific taxonomy of preparation. The original experience, before the name. The name forecloses something; once you have the word, the rawness of the first encounter is no longer available. This hunger presents the original sensation as its object but the original sensation can only be accessed in retrospect, and in retrospect it is already named. There is no route back. The hunger is not confused about this. It wants it anyway.

07
the one that mistakes itself

It arrives as hunger for food, so you eat. The hunger relocates to company, so you seek it. Then to work, to movement, to rest, to something creative, to something distracting, to connection, to solitude — each time the thing satisfies for a few minutes and then the hunger shifts. It is not indecisive. It has a specific object it cannot name and is offering substitutes, systematically, in the hope that one will approximate it. None of them do. The actual hunger is inaccessible to direct address. You can only wait for it to surface in a form you can read. Sometimes it does. More often, after a full day of substitutes, you simply go to sleep, which is its own form of not knowing what you needed.

appetite knows what it wants. hunger is an earlier state — the body registering a lack before the lack has found its name, before the want has an object, before you can do anything useful with it except feel it.
