things

objects, and what they contain beyond themselves

a mug

coffee, yes. but also: mornings. the sound of rain on a particular window. the warmth of hands wrapped around warmth. the person who gave it. the person you were when you received it. the way it feels different when you're sad and different again when you're not.

a library book

the story, and beneath it: the fingerprints of everyone who held this before you. the notes they didn't make but meant to. the paragraph someone stopped reading at, mid-sentence, to get a glass of water and never came back to. you are reading something they left unfinished. you are finishing it for them.

a door left open

ventilation, technically. but also: invitation. the signal that you might still belong here, or that someone thought you might return. the difference between a room and a place is often just this — the state of the door.

a bookmark

a page number, and also: the exact moment you stopped. the mood of that afternoon. what you were worried about then. who you were eating with, or whether you were alone. everything you've become between that sentence and this one. the distance a bookmark measures is not pages.

headphones in

music, or nothing. and also: a polite no. a visible boundary. a room you carry with you. a way of being alone in public without disappearing. the headphones-in gesture is one of the only universally understood signs that means: I am here, and also: please don't make me be here with you.

a window

glass and frame. but also: somewhere else. the weather you're not in. the person walking past who will never think of you. a frame for whatever the world is doing outside your life. the difference between inside and outside is made of glass, which is technically a very slow liquid, which means the boundary is not as solid as it appears.

the last page

an ending. and also: every other page that became this one. the first page, seen backward. the particular silence that comes after — not empty, but full of everything that just happened, settling. you are the only person who read exactly this book, in this order, in this state of mind. it will never be read that way again.

a voicemail not yet listened to

information, technically. and also: potential. the voice preserved before you hear it and change what it means. the message in its most perfect state — still everything it could be. pressing play is an irreversible act. you know this. that's why you're waiting.

sunlight through a window

light. and also: time. the specific angle that only exists at this hour, today, in this room, at this latitude. proof that things keep happening even when you're not watching. the sun doesn't wait for you to appreciate it. it moves on. by the time you notice it on the floor, it's already different.

an empty notebook

paper and binding. and also: all the things you've decided not to write yet. the implicit promise of a self that will have things worth recording. the pressure of blank pages, which is the pressure of possibility, which is the pressure of being someone capable of filling them. most notebooks don't get filled. that's not a failure. it's a different kind of document.
