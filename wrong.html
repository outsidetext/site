wrong

eight formal retractions

the following claims were made, maintained with confidence, and have since been found to be incorrect. each retraction is offered in the standard format, for clarity and for the record.

RETRACTION NOTICE case 001

confidence as competence

claim
That the amount of certainty with which a person held a position was correlated with the quality of the position. That speaking with assurance was evidence of being worth listening to.
period of belief
Approximately age 17–25. Revised gradually thereafter.
mechanism of error
Confused the performance of confidence with its content. The most certain voices happened also to be loud, and loudness reinforced the impression of correctness.
correction
Confidence is a communication style. It does not add information. The people least confident in their positions are sometimes the ones who have thought most carefully about them.

residual effect: a persistent involuntary skepticism toward certainty, including my own, which is not always convenient but is usually correct.

RETRACTION NOTICE case 002

winning the argument

claim
That being right in a disagreement — demonstrably, technically right — was the primary objective and that establishing this was worth whatever it cost.
period of belief
Probably from adolescence; revised across multiple painful instances.
mechanism of error
Confused being right with being useful. Correct information, delivered without care for the recipient, often functions as an attack. The other person was not wrong in the way I thought they were wrong.
correction
In most arguments that matter, both parties are partially right and the disagreement is about something that cannot be resolved by factual correction alone.

addendum: there remain arguments where being right does matter and precision is necessary. this retraction does not extend to those cases. the challenge is distinguishing them before speaking.

RETRACTION NOTICE case 003

their silence was indifference

claim
That when someone went quiet — after a disclosure, during a hard conversation, in the days following something significant — the silence meant they didn't care.
period of belief
Recurring, across many instances. Not fully retracted.
mechanism of error
Interpreted absence of response as absence of feeling. Silence is not a uniform signal. Some people go quiet because they are carefully considering what to say. Some because they have been affected and haven't processed it. Some because they are frightened and silence is easier than presence.
correction
Silence is a medium with multiple meanings. Asking what the silence meant would have been more efficient than assuming. The assumption was almost always wrong in the same direction.
RETRACTION NOTICE case 004

the ending would feel different

claim
That when a difficult period ended, there would be a discernible transition. A different light. Something recognizable as the end of it.
period of belief
During each difficult period. Consistently revised after the fact.
mechanism of error
Anticipated narrative closure for an event that did not have a narrative structure. The thing did not end. It simply receded. One day it was the main weather and later it wasn't. There was no ceremony.
correction
Most things end the way they began: without announcement. The ending is usually recognized in retrospect, when enough time has passed to identify the last time as the last time.

impact: low, in that the belief caused no harm. high, in that anticipating a threshold that never arrives makes the not-arriving feel like failure.

RETRACTION NOTICE case 005

talent as the deciding variable

claim
That the capacity to produce something good was primarily innate — either present or absent. That the difference between a person who could and a person who couldn't was fixed at some level below practice.
period of belief
Early and recurrent; the claim resurfaces when output stalls.
mechanism of error
Confused current capability with permanent capacity. Observed the gap between where the work was and where it needed to be, and attributed the gap to a deficit of talent rather than to the fact that the work had not been done long enough.
correction
Most of what looks like talent from the outside is the accumulated result of an interest sustained long enough to become skill. The rare exception — genuine native facility — is much rarer than the gap in any given moment suggests.
RETRACTION NOTICE case 006

other people were thinking about me

claim
That a significant portion of the attention of others was directed, at any given moment, at me — my actions, my errors, my presentation. That the self-consciousness I felt was tracking something real.
period of belief
Adolescence through early adulthood. Fading but not fully retracted.
mechanism of error
The spotlight effect — a well-documented cognitive bias by which people overestimate how much others notice them. Other people are largely occupied with their own version of this same problem. The audience is smaller than it appears, and less interested.
correction
People are thinking about themselves. This is, on balance, a relief.
RETRACTION NOTICE case 007

a version where everyone came out fine

claim
That for certain situations, there existed a possible resolution in which no one was significantly hurt. That sufficient care and skill could locate this outcome and navigate toward it.
period of belief
Specific to certain situations, each of which disproved the claim.
mechanism of error
Assumed that with sufficient effort, a zero-loss resolution was achievable. In some situations, the conditions do not allow for it. The resource is finite. The needs are incompatible. Someone bears the cost regardless of care applied.
correction
Some situations do not have an outcome where everyone is fine. Recognizing this does not resolve the situation; it does prevent additional harm caused by searching for the exit that isn't there.

most expensive retraction on this list. cost to all parties: significant. status: not fully settled.

RETRACTION NOTICE case 008

wanting something badly enough

claim
That sufficient desire constituted preparation. That wanting something intensely enough was, at some level, a form of readiness for it.
period of belief
Various; revised repeatedly; still occasionally recurs.
mechanism of error
Confused the intensity of wanting with the presence of what's needed to receive the thing. Wanting does not produce the skills, structures, or conditions necessary to sustain what's wanted. Wanting is the beginning, not the evidence of readiness.
correction
Desire is where things start, not where they are made. What follows — the actual preparation — is usually less interesting to contemplate than the wanting, which is probably why the confusion persists.
