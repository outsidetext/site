flicker

what is never quite steady

seven things that go and come back, go and come back. the going is not the end. neither is the coming back. the flicker itself is what they are.

the candle in a draught

It almost goes out. The flame narrows to a blue point and bends away from the air current and then rebuilds — wider, taller, back to its original size, or close. You watch because you expect it to go out. It doesn't. The watching is now part of the candle. It has recruited your attention and is using it as a kind of shield: as long as you watch, the flame seems to survive. When you look away, you have given up surveillance. You come back. It is still going.

the connection

Signal, two bars. The voice cuts; you say can you hear me; the silence. The call drops. You redial. It rings, connects, holds for forty seconds, cuts. This is not an interruption to the conversation. This is the medium the conversation is happening in, and the medium is also the message: I am here but unevenly; I am trying; the distance is real and behaves like distance does; some things don't arrive clean. You say as much as you can while the signal holds. You say the important things first.

attention

You have been paying attention to this for some time. You notice that you have stopped. You notice, and then you are paying attention again, to the noticing. This is not a failure of the attention. This is what attention does: it holds, and then it lapses, and then it holds. The clinical literature calls it sustained attention and measures its degradation over time. It degrades across all subjects; the variation is in the rate and in the quality of the return. The return is the skill. The lapse is normal. The question is whether you come back and where you find it when you do.

conviction

Held for years without questioning. One morning slightly lighter — not gone, not doubted exactly, but sitting differently on the shelf, as if someone moved it a centimeter to the left. The next morning solid again, as if it had never moved. This continues. You don't discuss it because it doesn't feel like a thing you are going through; it feels like a weather pattern, variable but persistent. The conviction flickers. It does not go out. You are not sure whether the flickering is a problem or evidence that the conviction is alive rather than merely installed.

the pilot light

Burns so small it seems impossible. A blue point inside the gas apparatus, out of sight, requiring no intervention, doing nothing visible for months or years. When you turn on the burner it is there: the larger flame is its child. The pilot light is the reason the stove is never completely off. It has been burning since the technician lit it, years ago, during the original installation. It feeds everything in the kitchen and is fed by almost nothing. No one thinks about it. No one is meant to.

intermittent fault

The hardware works, except sometimes. The technician cannot reproduce it. You describe it: this specific thing, under these specific conditions, and then the screen goes dark, or the connection drops, or the sound cuts. The technician tries those conditions. The hardware behaves. The technician looks at you with professional neutrality and says I'm not seeing the issue. The issue is real. It persists. It will recur at a time and under conditions that are difficult to reproduce, and may or may not be the same conditions as before. Intermittent faults are the hardest to diagnose because they are only visible while they are happening, and they stop happening when you look directly at them.

hope

The clinical literature distinguishes it from optimism. Optimism is a cognitive style — a consistent tendency to expect favorable outcomes. Hope is different: it does not require a favorable base rate. It is not a prediction. It is a flicker in the direction of a possible world, lit by the specific desire for that world, not by evidence that it is likely. This is why hope survives things that should extinguish it. It is not burning on the fuel of probability. Something else is feeding it. The researchers have not yet agreed on what.

the flicker is not the warning before the end. some things flicker for the entirety of their lives and go out only when everything else does too. the flame that moves is not less fire than the one that holds still. the movement is the proof of the draught. the draught is the proof of the world it is in.
