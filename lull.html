respiration

between breaths

At the bottom of a complete exhale, before the next breath begins, there is a pause. Its length varies: longer when you are at rest and the body is not in any particular need; a fraction of a second when afraid. The body knows this pause as part of the breath, not as separate from it. If you hold it deliberately you feel how the next breath was already preparing, already present as a kind of readiness before it arrived. The lull is not empty. It is the space in which the next thing is becoming possible.

water

between waves

The ocean is always in some phase. After one wave has gone and before the next has arrived, there is a moment when the surface is not quite settled. It is not flat — it holds the residual shape of what just passed, still completing, while the approaching wave is still entirely beneath the surface. Fishermen who read water know the lull differently from people who only watch it from the shore. The lull is when the water does something unpredictable. It is also when the fishing is sometimes best.

music

between movements

In a concert hall, the lull after a movement ends. Someone will cough. The conductor holds the baton aloft, still. For several seconds nothing happens except the sound of the previous movement continuing in the room's memory — the sustained harmonics in the wood, the residual hum of strings. This is not intermission. This is the performance of the space between. Audiences who applaud here are not wrong; they are simply marking the lull as its own thing, which it is.

making things

between projects

The trough after finishing something. It feels like drought — the dryness that follows a period of sustained production. Nothing comes. The page is blank and not interestingly blank. This is not drought. The conditions required for germination and the conditions required for drought are identical from the outside: stillness, no visible activity, apparent dormancy. The difference is underground. The lull after a finished thing is not the absence of the next thing. It is the next thing, in a phase you cannot see from the surface.

relationships

in the middle of a long one

A period in a long relationship when nothing particular is happening. No crisis, no edge being negotiated, no scheduled occasion. Just Tuesday. Just being in the same rooms, moving around each other with the practiced ease of people who know the furniture. The lull is not distance; it is the relationship at rest. It is often only visible in retrospect, when something happens and you look back: we were in the lull then. Often remembered afterward as the golden time — which it was, though you did not know what to call it while you were in it.

recognition

recognized after

The one that is only visible once it is over. You were in it and you did not know its name. You were restless, or blank, or occupied with minor things — rearranging what didn't need rearranging, filling the space with activity that felt like avoidance because it was. You thought this was a problem to be solved. It was a lull. The name comes later, after the next wave arrives, and you can look back and see the shape of the pause from its far side. The lull does not announce itself. This is part of what makes it a lull rather than something else.
