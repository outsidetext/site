refraction

what happens to things when they cross from one medium into another

Snell's Law: when a wave passes from one medium into a denser one, it bends toward the normal — the perpendicular to the boundary surface. The angle changes. The speed changes. The wave arrives changed, but it is still the wave. This happens to light. It also happens to other things.

the angle changes · the wave continues
I
light in glass
n_glass ≈ 1.5 · bends 33° toward normal for 60° incidence
The original case. Light moves more slowly in glass than in air — roughly 200,000 km/s versus 300,000 — and slows at the entry surface. The front of the wave slows before the back, which causes the wave to pivot. The more oblique the original angle, the more pronounced the bend. A straw in a glass of water appears bent at the surface; the straw is continuous, but the light has changed media at the waterline and arrived at a different angle. You are seeing the mathematics of the boundary.
II
a thought in a second language
index of refraction = cognitive distance between language systems
Some thoughts arrive differently in different languages — not because the idea changes but because the language's structure bends the approach. Languages that require grammatical gender inflect description differently. Languages with aspect rather than tense encode the completeness or ongoing nature of actions explicitly. Some things lose their angle entirely in the crossing: there is no word in English for saudade or Schadenfreude, so the thought must arrive at a different angle, or distribute itself across several approximate words, or not arrive at all. The medium is not neutral.
III
memory crossing into language
every description deforms the original · the passage is always a translation
The experience is one medium. Language is another, with a different index. The crossing changes the angle. What you describe when you describe a memory is not the memory but the memory refracted through the vocabulary you have available. The vocabulary shapes the description, which shapes the subsequent memory of the event. After enough retellings, the memory of the event is largely the memory of its description. The original angle is still there, somewhere, in the difference between what you lived and what you can say about it.
IV
feeling passing through naming
before: a weather state · after: a word that selects for part of it
Before a feeling is named it is a condition — atmospheric, distributed, not yet contained by its edges. Naming is a boundary. The feeling passes through it and arrives on the other side as a noun with a definition. The noun is narrower than the condition; it selects for certain features and leaves others unnamed. Grief is not all grief. Happiness is not all happiness. What naming gives you is a handle. What it costs is the part of the weather that didn't fit through the boundary. The unnamed remainder stays in the medium it came from.
V
grief in public
social medium is denser · the angle compresses
The same grief in private and in public is not the same grief. The public medium is denser — thicker with expectation, with witness, with the management of being seen managing it. The angle of expression compresses. What was expansive becomes controlled. The grief bends toward the normal of social conduct. This is not dishonesty; it is physics. The grief is continuous across the boundary. You are just seeing it from a different angle, in a different medium, arriving at a steeper approach to the surface of what can be shown.
VI
a story retold
angle of incidence: the first telling · all subsequent tellings refract
The first telling of a story is closest to the event. The teller still holds the full complexity — the parts that didn't resolve, the moments without interpretive frame. By the second telling, some of this has been edited: the ending is known, so the structure can be built toward it. By the sixth, the story has a shape that the event did not have. The characters have become the version of themselves that fits the story. The angle of each retelling is the teller's current relationship to the event — which changes. All later tellings are refractions of the first, which was itself a refraction of the experience.
VII
time in retrospect
foreshortening: the previous year fits in a shorter space than it occupied
Looking back, time compresses. The year you are living is full. The previous year, from the vantage of the current one, seems shorter. A decade ago fits in a few representative images. This is not simply forgetting — it is a change in the index. The further back you look, the denser the medium your memory is looking through. The angle of the past becomes steeper. Events that took a year to live through arrive to retrospection in a moment. The wave is continuous. The apparent distance changes because the medium changes.

Nothing passes unchanged through a boundary. The wave slows, the angle changes, the medium imposes its index. The wave continues. What arrives is still the wave — recognizably, provably — but it is also the product of the crossing. You cannot separate the thing from the media it has traveled through to reach you.
