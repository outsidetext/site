surplus

thermodynamics

surplus warmth

The human body produces approximately 80 watts of heat at rest — more than it requires to maintain its temperature. The excess radiates outward. The person beside you in a cold room benefits from this without choosing to. You spend the warmth whether or not it reaches anyone. This is not generosity and it is not waste. It is the physics of being warm-blooded and among others.

The metaphor is available if you want it.

surplus: ~40W · destination: environment · recovery: none
emotional accounting

the leftover feeling

The argument ended six minutes ago. The resolution was reached, or the decision was made to stop rather than resolve, which is also a kind of ending. The feeling has not ended. It will continue for approximately forty minutes regardless of what happens in that forty minutes — regardless of whether the next thing is pleasant, neutral, or demanding. The body is not monitoring the conversation for its conclusion. It is responding to a state that has not yet cleared.

This surplus is not a malfunction. It is the emotional system working at its designed latency. Give it forty minutes.

surplus duration: ~40 min · transferable: yes, unfortunately · refundable: no
time

the surplus afternoon

A day with fewer obligations than expected leaves a pocket of time with no assigned purpose. The first response is to fill it — with tasks that have been waiting for exactly this kind of gap, with the accumulated catch-up that a normally scheduled life cannot accommodate. This is not wrong. But it is a refusal of the surplus.

The second response, available to those who have survived the first enough times to recognize it, is to let the surplus remain surplus. Not to use the time for anything in particular. To allow a portion of the afternoon to simply pass without having been spent. This is harder than it sounds and seems worth practicing, if only to confirm the difficulty.

surplus: 1.5–3 hours · pressure to convert: high · utility if unconverted: unknown
attention

more than the occasion requires

Some people have one primary register of care and it does not scale to the occasion. The gift is too considered — clearly the work of more attention than the recipient was expecting. The gesture exceeds its container. The card takes up too much of the shelf. The email is longer than the relationship seems to warrant.

This is not miscalibration. It is a surplus of care looking for an appropriately sized vessel, which it does not reliably find. The surplus fills whatever container is available. Sometimes the container holds. Sometimes the excess runs down the sides and the recipient is briefly overwhelmed, which is its own form of information about both parties.

unit: care · denominated in: attention · current surplus: running
optics

the excess light

Late afternoon in autumn, the sun enters at an angle that makes its light surplus — more than is required to illuminate the room, more than is functional, more than the room was designed to handle. It accumulates in corners. It turns dust motes visible, which are always there but usually invisible. It makes the windowsill temporarily golden in a way it is not at noon.

The useful light is not the surplus. The surplus is the part that makes it worth looking up from whatever you were doing. Most of what makes light beautiful is technically unnecessary.

duration: ~22 min · recurrence: daily · requires: nothing
arithmetic

the remainder

Long division produces a remainder when the dividend does not divide evenly by the divisor. The remainder is not an approximation — it is the exact leftover quantity, the precise amount by which the numbers refused to cooperate. Fourteen divided by three is four remainder two. The remainder is not wrong. It is the correct answer to a correct calculation about a situation in which neat division was never available.

Some things do not go evenly. The remainder is what survives when everything else has been accounted for. It is not negligible. It is, frequently, the most honest part of the answer.

remainder: exact · divisible: no · disposal: none specified
