half-life

how quickly feelings decay

A radioactive substance decays at a constant rate: after one half-life, half remains; after two, a quarter. Feelings do not follow this rule. But they follow something like it.

the fast-moving

feelings that move through in hours

the specific embarrassment of a social misstep 11 min (acute); recurring the acute flush passes in minutes. the memory visits later, at night, without warning.
anger from a small frustration 18–45 minutes longer if the object of the anger is present or discussed. shorter if there is nowhere to put it.
the mood set by a song 15 min – 3 hours duration correlates with how many times you listen on repeat.
the self-consciousness of being observed 20–50 minutes extends significantly if the observer was someone whose opinion carries weight.
delight at an unexpected small thing 2–8 hours among the most unstable of the positive states. dissipates faster with distraction.

the slow ones

feelings that stay through the night and into the next day

relief after a deadline 4–12 hours often mistaken for happiness. it is the absence of a weight, not the presence of a thing.
longing (activated by a trigger) 2–7 days recurs. each recurrence resets the clock approximately. the half-life does not compound.
joy from significant good news 3–5 days before becoming ordinary hedonic adaptation is not a malfunction. it is how the system makes room.
the homesickness of the first weeks somewhere new 10–21 days highly variable. familiarity accelerates decay. some strains are resistant.

the ones that don’t decay

no half-life observed; classified separately

grief (transitioning from acute to chronic) 6–18 months (acute phase only) the acute phase decays. what remains after is not absence — it is a different substance entirely.
the knowledge that something is over does not decay reclassified. not a feeling with a half-life but a permanent update to the record. present in all subsequent readings.
a love that has since changed form half-life not applicable does not disappear; transforms. the substance is conserved even as the state changes. chemistry, not physics.

Unlike radioactive decay, emotional half-life is not constant across individuals or contexts. The same grief, revisited, may have a different rate. Attention changes the rate. Memory resets the clock. These measurements are approximate and personal, which means they may not be yours.
