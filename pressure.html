known weather systems

Certain atmospheric patterns recur with enough regularity to be named. These are not the same as moods. Moods are short-duration phenomena. Weather systems are structural — they move in, they stay, they move out on their own schedule.

  • 1023 mb
    high clarity Good visibility in all directions. The kind of day where decisions feel possible. Duration: variable. Can hold for hours or days. Not guaranteed to last.
  • 1013 mb
    standard conditions The baseline. Most days. Things proceed at approximately their normal rate. Sometimes this is enough; sometimes it isn't. Both assessments are valid.
  • 1001 mb
    variable pressure front Fluctuating conditions. Characteristic of late afternoon and Sunday evenings. The system has not decided what it is yet. This is uncomfortable. It usually resolves by morning.
  • 990 mb
    low approaching Pressure dropping. Nothing specific yet, just the sense that something is on the way. Best to prepare without panicking. Often arrives less severely than the drop suggested.
  • 978 mb
    deep low Sustained low pressure system. Visibility reduced. The weather is what it is and will be for a while. These systems are not personal. They have their own duration.
  • 968 mb
    the small hours front Characteristic of 2–4am. Pressure at minimum. Things seem more absolute than they are. Decisions made in this system should be reviewed in daylight. Duration: finite. The pressure always rises.
