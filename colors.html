colors

names for colors that don't have them

3am grey
#c2c8d8
The color of a room lit only by a phone screen at three in the morning. Not quite silver. Not quite blue. The color of being awake when you shouldn't be, looking at light that was made for daylight hours.
lamp yellow
#f5d48a
The specific warmth of a lamp left on in an empty room. Yellow, but softer. The color of a house you walk past at night and briefly, without reason, want to live inside.
first light
#fce8e2
Not sunrise — the moment before, when the sky hasn't decided yet. Barely pink. The color of potential, or of something about to be irrevocably changed.
old photograph
#c9a87a
The specific brown of sepia — not quite any natural color. The color of memory filtered through time, and the strange warmth that gives to things that were perhaps not warm at all.
rain on concrete
#8a9097
The grey that darkens slowly from the edges in. The color of waiting for something to pass, and the particular stillness that settles when you accept that you are going to get wet.
end of summer
#e8a840
The gold that light turns in late August when it knows something you don't. Too warm to be yellow. Too sad to be beautiful. The color of things that are ending so slowly you can only see it in retrospect.
the internet at night
#2a3a5c
The blue of a screen seen through a window from outside. Cold, with faint intelligence inside it. The color of being watched from a distance and not knowing whether to wave.
hospital waiting
#e8ede8
A particular institutional off-white with a trace of green. The color of patience that has left the body. The color rooms turn when people have been sitting in them too long, hoping.
almost home
#d4a876
The particular quality of light when you're five minutes from somewhere you love. Nothing changes. Something does. The color of anticipation that's close enough to touch.
the depths of a good book
#4a3728
The near-dark that settles around you when you've been reading for hours and forgot where you are. Not darkness. Absorption. The color of being elsewhere in a room you know very well.
forgotten voicemail
#b8c4d0
The muted blue-grey of a notification that's been there so long you've stopped seeing it. Urgency that has given up. A message waiting patiently to be heard or permanently ignored.
inbox zero
#f0f4f0
The brief, impossible white of a cleared inbox. More absence than color. The color of a task completed, a breath held, a state that will not last. Enjoy it. It won't last.
