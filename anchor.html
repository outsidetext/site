anchor

what holds things in place

the seafloor kind

A ship's anchor does not stop the boat from moving. It controls the radius of the drift. The boat will still swing with tide and current; the anchor determines how far it can go. Drop the anchor in fifteen feet of water and let out sixty feet of chain and the boat can swing in a circle sixty feet across — not held, but tethered. A gale can drag an anchor across the bottom. Being anchored is not the same as being fixed. It is being fixed enough: constrained within an acceptable range, given the conditions, for the duration you expect to need to stay.

— on the difference between fixity and sufficient stability

the cognitive kind

The first number introduced into a negotiation becomes the anchor. It does not matter how arbitrary it is — a randomly generated figure, a number from an unrelated context, a price tag glimpsed in passing. Subsequent estimates adjust from the anchor insufficiently. This is not irrationality in the pejorative sense; it is a feature of a mind that conserves energy by starting from what is already present. The problem is that what is already present was not chosen for relevance. The anchor was chosen by whoever spoke first, or by whatever you happened to see. This person is almost never you.

— on what we adjust from and why we don't adjust far enough

the memory kind

There is one instance of each thing that all subsequent instances are measured against. Often not the best or worst, but the first — the first time you understood that something was possible. All subsequent versions are assessed by their distance from this anchor, which is not fair to the subsequent versions but is unavoidable. The anchor memory does not have to be good to function as an anchor. A first apartment can be cramped and badly lit and still serve as the standard against which all other apartments are quietly measured. The anchor is not a model. It is just the first coordinate from which the map was drawn.

— on the first instance as the baseline

the person kind

The person you call when you need to be located. Not because they always have the answer — they frequently do not — but because the specific weight of their voice in a particular register returns you to yourself. Something about the cadence, the pace of their thinking, the way they ask the next question. After the conversation you know where you are again; before it you did not. You may not tell them they serve this function. They may serve it without knowing they serve it. The anchoring does not require the anchor's awareness. It requires only their availability and their specific gravity.

— on the people who locate us without being asked to

the word kind

The word at the center of a thought — the one everything else is moored to. If you find it, the rest of the sentence is recoverable: it will follow the anchor word into the light. If you cannot find it, the whole sentence drifts. You can see the shape of the sentence, the approximate location of the idea, the words on either side of the gap — but without the anchor word the sentence will not hold together long enough to be written down. Writing is mostly the search for anchor words. You work around the center until the center appears, and then the sentence snaps into place, and you wonder why it took so long when it was that word all along.

— on what holds a thought together

an anchor does not resist movement. it defines the limit of movement. it says: this far, and within this radius, and back here when the current changes. the ship still swings with the weather. being anchored is not the same as being still.
