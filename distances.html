distances

measuring the unmeasurable

01
The distance between knowing and understanding. eleven years, on average. sometimes longer. sometimes the trip never completes.
02
The distance between two people sitting in the same room, not speaking and two people sitting in the same room, not needing to. immeasurable and precise. you know which one you're in.
03
The distance between where you are and where you feel at home. not always measured in miles.
04
The distance between a thought and a word. sometimes nothing. sometimes everything. the same thought, different days.
05
The distance between the last thing you said to someone and the last thing you meant to say. variable. the gap is sometimes a sentence. sometimes years.
06
The distance between how old you feel and how old you are. fluctuates wildly. largest on monday mornings and certain birthdays.
07
The distance between the version of someone you carry in your memory and who they are now. grows with every year you don't see them. you are both still growing.
08
The distance between being heard and being understood. wider than most conversations bridge. narrower than most people believe.
09
The distance between 3am and the same problem in the morning. six hours. the problem is often smaller at 9am. not always, but often.
10
The distance between who you were going to be and who you are. impossible to determine from inside the situation. this is not a failure. this is just perspective.
11
The distance between wanting to say something and saying it. for most people, for most things that matter: too far. cross it anyway.
12
The distance between the way a place looks in your memory and how it looks when you return. the place has not moved. something else has.
13
The distance between tired and the kind of tired that sleep doesn't fix. recognisable by feel. the second one needs something else.
14
The distance between knowing what the right thing to do is and doing it. the most interesting gap in human experience. the gap where all the stories live.
15
The distance between the internet and the world. shrinking rapidly. unclear whether this is good.
16
The distance between what you meant and what arrived. determined by medium, timing, the receiver's mood, and what they already knew about you.
17
The distance between being alone and being lonely. varies. sometimes they are the same place. sometimes they are opposite ends of the room.
18
The distance between the last time you saw someone and the moment you realised it was the last time. you almost never know it at the time. this is probably for the best.
